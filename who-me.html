Who me?

A marketer, a storyteller, a builder of brands — and the journey that shaped it all.

Chapter Zero

The Disclaimer

Before we get into the impressive stuff, a few things you should know:

This person once spent three hours perfecting a font pairing that nobody noticed. She has strong opinions about Oxford commas. Her Spotify Wrapped is 90% podcast episodes about branding. She has never, not once, posted an Instagram story without checking the preview at least four times.

She once cried over a Canva template. It was that ugly.

If any of that sounds relatable — or deeply concerning — keep scrolling. It only gets better from here.

"You've been warned."
Chapter One

The Roots

It started in Indore, a city in central India known for its street food and its spirit. Between 2010 and 2012, a curious mind earned a Bachelor of Business Administration from Devi Ahilya Vishwavidyalaya — learning the language of business, strategy, and the art of getting things done.

But classrooms could only contain so much ambition. By 2013, a deeper fascination took hold — one with live experiences, crowd energy, and the magic of events. A Post Graduate Diploma in Event Management and Public Relations from the Prestige Institute of Management and Research became the bridge between knowing about business and actually creating moments that people remember.

"She graduated before Instagram had Reels. Let that sink in."
Indore, India · 2010 – 2014
Chapter Two

The Brand Builder

In 2015, the real world came calling. As a Brand Manager at Brand Catapult, the next five years became a masterclass in digital storytelling — crafting campaigns for Gen Z and Millennial audiences, building content calendars from scratch, and watching brands grow from quiet names into recognizable voices.

Every scroll, every post, every piece of data told a story. Social media wasn't just a channel — it was a canvas. Monitoring trends, refining strategy, pushing creative limits — this was the daily rhythm, and it never got old.

"Five years of telling brands to 'be authentic' while ghostwriting their captions."
Brand Catapult, India · 2015 – 2020
Chapter Three

Going Solo

Then came the leap. In 2020, while the world was pressing pause, Social Shake was born — a marketing venture built from the ground up. As founder and lead strategist, the mission was clear: help brands find their voice in the noise.

Directing multiple projects simultaneously. Defining scopes, timelines, and budgets. Leading cross-functional teams of designers, writers, and marketers. Working with finance stakeholders to keep things lean and efficient. Three years of building something from nothing — and proving that great work doesn't need a big name behind it.

3 Years as Founder
10+ Clients Served
1 Bold Leap
"Started a company during a pandemic. Because clearly, one crisis wasn't enough."
Social Shake (Founder) · 2020 – 2023
Chapter Four

A New Chapter

In September 2023, the story crossed oceans. Boston. Northeastern University. A Master of Science in Project Management at the College of Professional Studies — blending everything learned so far with new frameworks in scope, schedule, cost, quality, and risk.

The coursework reads like a marketer's dream layered with a project manager's precision: business analysis, professional communication, ethics, negotiation, digital marketing analytics, branding strategy, and consumer behavior.

"Moved 8,000 miles for a degree. Some people just can't take a hint to sit still."
Northeastern University, Boston · 2023 – Present
Chapter Five

The Present

Today, the story writes itself across two roles at Northeastern — each one a different shade of the same drive.

As Marketing and Communications Coordinator at the School of Criminology and Criminal Justice, the numbers speak for themselves. Multi-channel campaigns across LinkedIn, Instagram, and X — each one aligned with real departmental goals. And a research website built from scratch.

As a Social Media Manager through NU Temps, the work continued — leading end-to-end campaigns, managing content calendars, and turning organizational objectives into scroll-stopping content.

Boston, MA · Now
& Beyond
